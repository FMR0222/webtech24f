Community Service Journey

Empowering communities through social impact

Inspired by Family Values

Growing up in a family where giving back was the norm, community service became a natural part of my life. With both my parents heavily involved in community initiatives—my mother being a psychologist and my father contributing through various channels,I was always exposed to the idea of giving more than you take. Early on, I participated in donating clothes and distributing food to the less fortunate.

School and Community Projects

My involvement deepened as I grew older. In school, I joined various volunteer activities, including the "Hand in Hand" initiative, which focused on refurbishing underfunded schools. Additionally, projects like LOYAC allowed me to make a difference back home, instilling in me a deeper sense of purpose.

Babson's Emphasis on Social Impact

The emphasis Babson places on social impact was a major reason for my choice to study there—besides its well-known entrepreneurial focus. I am also part of the Natalie Taylor Scholar Program, which has only strengthened my commitment to serving communities. Babson's dedication to social entrepreneurship has given me a platform to take my community work to new heights.
